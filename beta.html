20 JulData Sharing Agreements still paused behind the contracting trail; a session to be arranged rather than more email

The Data Sharing Agreement position has not moved and is now the thing holding the DPIA. NHS Legal will not progress the DSAs until satisfied on the contracting done to date, specifically the intellectual property and information governance provisions spread across the ACE contracts and the MoU, and has asked to see the underlying documents and the supplier and commissioner data protection arrangements sitting behind them. The 21 July board pack states plainly that NHS Legal is advising on the requirement for separate DSAs and logs the DSAs as dependent on PTT and NHS Legal advice and support, with the DPIA sign-off itself dependent on the MoU being in place with commissioners and the ACE contracts with suppliers.

The programme position on the substance is unchanged. Data Sharing Agreements here are controller to controller, between NHS England as controller of the platform and the commissioners and suppliers as controllers for their own purposes, which is a different instrument from the data processing agreements the suppliers and commissioners hold between themselves. Those processing arrangements are the counterparties' own obligation. The MoU wording places that obligation on them and reserves an audit and spot-check right, deliberately rather than routinely requesting sight of them, because routinely holding them would pull liability back onto NHS England and does not scale. The inconsistencies being picked over between the ACE contracts and the MoU exist because the contracting was done in stages as the picture firmed up, and the current MoU uplift is the attempt to bring the IG position up to date, alongside the IP addendums.

Two things were agreed on 20 July. First, to stop working this over email and get a session with Lexi Moffatt to settle the approach directly, with Shirley Gaynor-Ward offering to help push it along. Second, that the DPIA should not simply sit and wait behind the DSA advice; whether it can progress in parallel is being tested. Rachel Flower is picking the legal thread up with Wendi Higgins.

The wider point recorded alongside it is that there is currently no one in either the legal or the assurance team engaging on the future state IG position, as distinct from private beta, which leaves the assumptions about how the service will be governed at scale unexamined. That is logged as an issue on the delivery register.

14 JulSalford private beta measures of success drafted (working draft)

A working draft of the measures of success for the Salford private beta was filed on 14 July. Data to capture includes the drop-off point in the journey, invite-to-order and order-to-completion timings, stated barrier versus silent drop-off, and recruitment route against outcome.
