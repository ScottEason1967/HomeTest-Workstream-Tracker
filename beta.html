    The programme position on the substance is unchanged. Data Sharing Agreements here are controller to controller, between NHS England as controller of the platform and the commissioners and suppliers as controllers for their own purposes, which is a different instrument from the data processing agreements the suppliers and commissioners hold between themselves. Those processing arrangements are the counterparties' own obligation. The MoU wording places that obligation on them and reserves an audit and spot-check right, deliberately rather than routinely requesting sight of them, because routinely holding them would pull liability back onto NHS England and does not scale. The inconsistencies being picked over between the ACE contracts and the MoU exist because the contracting was done in stages as the picture firmed up, and the current MoU uplift is the attempt to bring the IG position up to date, alongside the IP addendums.
