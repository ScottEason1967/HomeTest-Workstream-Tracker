29 JunRDUH site-visit notes filed: prior PSA pilot numbers and device direction

The prior RDUH PSA pilot ran a post-radical-prostatectomy pathway on capillary sampling built on Epic, with 917 invited, 310 sign-ups and 218 completed, around 60 per cent wanting to continue. The learning: one-to-one support for the first test and urology-team buy-in drive uptake, while PSA itself lends well to capillary with a low failure rate. Next steps sequenced as urology workshop, then pilot workshop, then Tasso-versus-Impress user research, then a wider-tests workshop. The pathway leans on a transdermal device, Impress preferred (FDA-approved, awaiting UKCA, MHRA timeline unclear and possibly end of year) with Tasso as fallback. Cerner/Epic integration complexity flagged for any RDUH-to-NW London (Chel West) multi-trust pilot.

Source: OS notes RDUH visit 23 June 2026.docx, Transcripts and meeting notes / Acute Initiated Discovery / RDUH.
