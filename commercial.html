20 JulProgramme Board received the commercial recommendation but could not approve it; the route now runs through Rob McMillan

The HomeTest Programme Board met on Monday 20 July, 15:00 to 16:00, chaired by Polly Bishop. The recommended direction was recorded as option B: NHS England operates the platform and the assurance standards while delivering a procurement vehicle through which commissioners purchase services from pre-assured suppliers connected to the platform. 20 JulProgramme finance: £8m budget, forecast around £200k under, with the interim pay split still to settle

Shirley Gaynor-Ward gave the board a finance position drawn from discussions with finance. The programme has an overall budget of £8 million, comprising £2 million revenue and £6 million capital, covering suppliers (Kainos and test suppliers), interim pay and wider DPSP costs.

Against the full-year budget the programme would initially have appeared around £200k over. Once committed spend and forecast outturn were reviewed the position reversed. For Kainos, the budget always allowed for the majority of revenue spend to fall in the first quarter given the nature of the work, with most other activity expected to be capitalised, and that remains the expected position with a year-to-date variance around £20k lower than anticipated. The forecast outturn still assumes the available funding is spent, taking account of the additional activities discussed by the programme. Because elements run on time and materials, the board noted there remains scope to claw funding back if the additional third use case work does not proceed or is later considered inappropriate to continue. On test suppliers, the forecast outturn is slightly lower than originally anticipated, identified as another area where funding may be clawed back.

The live pressure is interim pay. The programme originally operated under one cost code and has since moved across two, creating cross-charging complexity, and interim pay costs have been assumed to split between HealthStore and HomeTest. Shirley stated that if the costs cannot be split and are instead charged entirely to HomeTest, that creates a cost pressure that would need to be quantified. Taking everything together the current forecast outturn is around £200k under budget, which Polly Bishop asked to be recorded as the team's best current understanding. The board noted the position should be read in the context of the transition to the At Home and Community portfolio, the forthcoming DPSP strategic portfolio review, and Steven Dodd's work with Shirley Gaynor-Ward on the DPSP delivery transition plan.

13 JulFuture-state discussion paper drafted (v0.2): platform framed as national testing infrastructure

The 8 July future-state workshop has been written up as a discussion paper for programme alignment (v0.2), circulated for comment rather than as a recommendation on final commercial structure. It reframes the Home Test Platform as national testing infrastructure rather than a procurement for home-testing suppliers: the NHS App as the single patient front door, an NHS-controlled orchestration layer behind it, and multiple interoperable delivery models across NHS, independent and mixed provision. The central argument is to separate fair procurement for current services from active market shaping, aggregating fragmented demand and lowering shared fixed costs rather than predetermining who wins work or disguising a policy preference for NHS delivery, while recognising the pathology market is already mixed across roughly 28 networks. On framework breadth it proposes three distinct approvals, onboarding an organisation for capability, approving a test for clinical and laboratory suitability, and activating a pathway only when the end-to-end home-testing service is ready, held together by a standing supplier and catalogue management function so new tests can be admitted without reopening the vehicle. It argues the value should be built bottom-up test by test and pathway by pathway with low, central and high scenarios, on the view that the reasonably foreseeable opportunity could approach several hundred million pounds and should not be procured as if the first tranche were the whole ambition. It also sets minimum terms for tactical interim solutions (data portability, interoperability, exit and migration, non-exclusivity, IP, open-book), a reverse-plan from a defined operational need date and drop-dead date, and emergency and pandemic reuse to explore with UKHSA.
