13 JulFuture-state discussion paper drafted (v0.2): platform framed as national testing infrastructure

The 8 July future-state workshop has been written up as a discussion paper for programme alignment (v0.2), circulated for comment rather than as a recommendation on final commercial structure. It reframes the Home Test Platform as national testing infrastructure rather than a procurement for home-testing suppliers: the NHS App as the single patient front door, an NHS-controlled orchestration layer behind it, and multiple interoperable delivery models across NHS, independent and mixed provision. The central argument is to separate fair procurement for current services from active market shaping, aggregating fragmented demand and lowering shared fixed costs rather than predetermining who wins work or disguising a policy preference for NHS delivery, while recognising the pathology market is already mixed across roughly 28 networks. On framework breadth it proposes three distinct approvals, onboarding an organisation for capability, approving a test for clinical and laboratory suitability, and activating a pathway only when the end-to-end home-testing service is ready, held together by a standing supplier and catalogue management function so new tests can be admitted without reopening the vehicle. It argues the value should be built bottom-up test by test and pathway by pathway with low, central and high scenarios, on the view that the reasonably foreseeable opportunity could approach several hundred million pounds and should not be procured as if the first tranche were the whole ambition. It also sets minimum terms for tactical interim solutions (data portability, interoperability, exit and migration, non-exclusivity, IP, open-book), a reverse-plan from a defined operational need date and drop-dead date, and emergency and pandemic reuse to explore with UKHSA. The paper is explicit that the next several weeks are commercial and operating-model design, not a procurement-writing exercise, feeding the sourcing strategy.

8 JulProcurement planning and sourcing strategy workshop, Leeds

The procurement planning and sourcing strategy workshop ran in Leeds on 8 July, following on from the future-state recommendations session two weeks earlier. James Cowley set out the base-case procurement approach as a single-stage open procedure: an open framework any supplier can bid for, with one compliant bid accepted, and commissioners able to direct-award off it once it is live. The alternative on the table is a competitive flexible procedure, adding stages such as conditions of participation and a negotiation round to sharpen pricing, at the cost of a longer timeline. On who administers the framework, James reported early soundings: NHS SPS is running at around two years for a process and is too slow; LPP is at around thirteen months with discussions opened; and early conversations with the NHS supply chain team were lukewarm. Richard Samuel steered the framework go-live from August to September. To bridge the gap before the framework is live, the group discussed a tactical interim using the existing CDC fifteen-pound blood-draw tariff for 2026/27, on the view that several kits could be delivered for less than that rate. Proper engagement with the pathology networks was deliberately sequenced to September so their route into the market can be set out first. The end-September working target for the sourcing strategy was carried forward, and the afternoon covered future-state lessons for HealthStore.

Source: HomeTest procurement planning and sourcing strategy workshop, Leeds, 8 July 2026.
