    Sourcing cornerstones

    OwnerRachel Flower / Scott Eason with Shirley Gaynor-Ward NextCornerstones foundation workshop, 13 Aug Depends onNamed programme owners; volumes to set the value ReferenceDelivery register risk, RAG 16, critical date 13 Aug

    The six interdependent questions that drive the sourcing strategy: ITT specification, volume and value, commercial models, the interim route, lotting and the go-live date. They are the focus of the workshop programme through August and September, and most still have no named programme owner, the RAG-16 risk tabled at the 21 July Delivery Board.

    Dated record
    1 · ITT specification

    The programme requirements that go into the tender. Fed by the sourcing strategy and the ITT-specification activities in stage one, running through August and September. Owner to be named.

    2 · Volume and value (the gating one)

    The critical input: it sets the framework value, which sets the governance route, possibly a full business case, and it sets the go-live date, so the approval route and volumes sit on the same critical path. Named at the 25 June Delivery Board as a critical dependency and restated on 21 July as the critical input; without it the governance-ready sourcing strategy cannot be approved. Fed by the 5/4/4 prioritisation, volumes, clinical proportion, buyer identification and pathology architecture. It is built from the NHS Online pathways with sensitivity ranges and carries ministerial visibility on the board risk slide; Matt Thomas is coordinating, with the health economist to give the confidence level on what drives the volumes.

    3 · Commercial models

    Pricing structure, tariff design and payment flow. Fed by the pricing-model direction and health-economist engagement. Tracked in more depth on the commercial model and tariff items.

    4 · Interim route to market

    The commissioner-led interim (Plan C) via existing frameworks that protects the April 2027 Beta while the open framework is built. Fed by the Plan C track: cohort, playbook, SBS bridge, NHS Online and the IDA plan. Tracked in full on the interim route item.

    5 · Lotting strategy

    How the requirement divides into lots. Fed by the 5/4/4 prioritisation, volumes, clinical proportion and buyer identification; the content shifts depending on which value scenario lands. Owner to be named.

    6 · Go-live date

    Fed by the sourcing-strategy sign-off, the route lock, framework administration and the approvals stack. The indicative framework go-live is 1 August 2027. Owner to be named.

    How they interlink

    No single answer stands on its own. The 5/4/4 prioritisation decides which use cases come next, which sets the lotting structure, drives buyer identification and shapes volume and value; volume and value in turn set the commercial model and the approval route; clinical proportion sets the regime defensibility (PA23 versus PSR); framework administration runs in parallel and still bears on the go-live date. That interdependence is why volume and value is the gating cornerstone.

    13 JulFuture-state discussion paper drafted (v0.2): platform framed as national testing infrastructure

    The 8 July future-state workshop has been written up as a discussion paper for programme alignment (v0.2), circulated for comment rather than as a recommendation on final commercial structure. It reframes the Home Test Platform as national testing infrastructure rather than a procurement for home-testing suppliers: the NHS App as the single patient front door, an NHS-controlled orchestration layer behind it, and multiple interoperable delivery models across NHS, independent and mixed provision. The central argument is to separate fair procurement for current services from active market shaping, aggregating fragmented demand and lowering shared fixed costs rather than predetermining who wins work or disguising a policy preference for NHS delivery, while recognising the pathology market is already mixed across roughly 28 networks. On framework breadth it proposes three distinct approvals, onboarding an organisation for capability, approving a test for clinical and laboratory suitability, and activating a pathway only when the end-to-end home-testing service is ready, held together by a standing supplier and catalogue management function so new tests can be admitted without reopening the vehicle. It argues the value should be built bottom-up test by test and pathway by pathway with low, central and high scenarios, on the view that the reasonably foreseeable opportunity could approach several hundred million pounds and should not be procured as if the first tranche were the whole ambition. It also sets minimum terms for tactical interim solutions (data portability, interoperability, exit and migration, non-exclusivity, IP, open-book), a reverse-plan from a defined operational need date and drop-dead date, and emergency and pandemic reuse to explore with UKHSA. The paper is explicit that the next several weeks are commercial and operating-model design, not a procurement-writing exercise, feeding the sourcing strategy.
